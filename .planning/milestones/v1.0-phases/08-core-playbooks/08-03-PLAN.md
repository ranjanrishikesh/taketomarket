**## Production Guidance** -- How to write email content. Cover:
- Subject line craft: 30-60 characters, no spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now, urgent, limited time, congratulations, winner), no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation (!!!, ???). Personalization tokens acceptable.
- Preview text: 40-130 characters, distinct from subject line, extends the subject's promise.
- Structure: single-column layout, text-dominant (60:40 text-to-image minimum), CTA above the fold.
- Dark mode compatibility: use transparent backgrounds or safe dark colors. Never rely on background images for readability. All text must be readable on both light and dark backgrounds.
- Deliverability: two dimensions. (1) Content-side: word choice, image ratio, formatting -- checked by DISC-EMAIL-01 through DISC-EMAIL-06. (2) DNS-side: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records must be properly configured for the sending domain -- checked by DISC-EMAIL-07 via automated DNS lookups per D-11. These are publicly queryable records; the AI verifier runs dig/nslookup commands at verify time.
- Compliance by default: every email must include unsubscribe link and physical mailing address (CAN-SPAM/GDPR requirement).

**## Discipline Gates** -- 7 gates (per D-11, includes DNS deliverability as an automated gate):

DISC-EMAIL-01: Subject/Preview Spam Scan -- Tier 1
- Checks: subject line and preview text for spam trigger patterns
- Against: known spam trigger word list
- Criteria 1: Spam triggers (PASS: no spam trigger words found in subject or preview, WARN: 1 borderline trigger word present with mitigating context, FAIL: 2+ spam trigger words OR ALL CAPS subject OR excessive punctuation)
- Criteria 2: Subject formatting (PASS: no ALL CAPS words, no more than 1 exclamation mark, no $$ or percentage-off claims in subject, WARN: one minor formatting issue, FAIL: multiple formatting red flags)

DISC-EMAIL-02: Dark Mode Rendering -- Tier 2
- Checks: content compatibility with dark mode email clients
- Against: dark mode rendering best practices
- Criteria 1: Background safety (PASS: no hard-coded light backgrounds that would create white blocks in dark mode, or explicit dark-mode-safe alternatives provided, WARN: background colors used but may render poorly -- manual dark mode test recommended, FAIL: email relies on background images for readability or uses white backgrounds without dark mode alternatives)
- Criteria 2: Image handling (PASS: all images have descriptive alt text and email is readable without images, WARN: images have alt text but email depends on images for key information, FAIL: image-only email with no alt text or text fallback)

DISC-EMAIL-03: Unsubscribe Presence -- Tier 1
- Checks: legal compliance elements in email
- Against: CAN-SPAM Act and GDPR requirements
- Criteria 1: Unsubscribe mechanism (PASS: unsubscribe or opt-out link clearly present and easy to find, WARN: unsubscribe present but buried or in very small text, FAIL: no unsubscribe link present)
- Criteria 2: Physical address (PASS: physical mailing address present (required by CAN-SPAM), WARN: address present but incomplete, FAIL: no physical address present)

DISC-EMAIL-04: Content-to-Image Ratio -- Tier 2
- Checks: text-to-image balance for deliverability
- Against: deliverability best practices (60:40 text dominant)
- Criteria 1: Text dominance (PASS: text content is clearly dominant -- email is fully readable with images blocked, WARN: roughly equal text and image content, FAIL: image-heavy email -- more image area than text, or image-only email)
- Criteria 2: Alt text coverage (PASS: every image has descriptive alt text, WARN: some images missing alt text, FAIL: no alt text on images)

DISC-EMAIL-05: Subject Line Length -- Tier 2
- Checks: subject line and preview text length optimization
- Against: email client display limits
- Criteria 1: Subject length (PASS: 30-60 characters, WARN: 20-29 or 61-80 characters, FAIL: under 20 or over 80 characters)
- Criteria 2: Preview text (PASS: preview text present and 40-130 characters and distinct from subject, WARN: preview text present but too short, too long, or repeats subject, FAIL: no preview text specified)

DISC-EMAIL-06: Single Clear CTA -- Tier 2
- Checks: CTA clarity and prominence
- Against: email content structure
- Criteria 1: Primary CTA (PASS: one clear primary CTA above the fold with action-oriented button text, WARN: CTA present but below the fold or uses weak text like "Click here", FAIL: no CTA or multiple competing CTAs of equal visual weight)
- Criteria 2: CTA hierarchy (PASS: if secondary CTAs exist they are visually subordinate to primary, WARN: secondary CTAs are nearly as prominent as primary, FAIL: N/A -- linked to primary CTA presence)

DISC-EMAIL-07: DNS Deliverability -- Tier 1 (per D-11)
- Checks: sending domain has SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records properly configured. These are public DNS records that can be queried programmatically at verify time.
- Against: DNS TXT records for the sending domain specified in the campaign brief or email asset metadata
- Criteria 1: SPF record (PASS: `dig TXT {domain}` returns a valid SPF record (v=spf1) that includes the email service provider's sending servers, WARN: SPF record exists but uses `+all` (overly permissive) or is missing the ESP's include directive, FAIL: no SPF TXT record found for the sending domain)
- Criteria 2: DKIM record (PASS: `dig TXT {selector}._domainkey.{domain}` returns a valid DKIM public key record (v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p=...), WARN: DKIM record exists but uses a weak key (< 1024 bits) or selector cannot be determined from brief, FAIL: no DKIM record found for the expected selector. Note: selector is typically provided by the ESP -- check brief for ESP name and use their default selector, e.g., "google" for Google Workspace, "k1" for Mailchimp, "s1" for SendGrid.)
- Criteria 3: DMARC record (PASS: `dig TXT _dmarc.{domain}` returns a DMARC policy record with p=quarantine or p=reject, WARN: DMARC record exists but uses p=none (monitoring only -- not enforcing), FAIL: no DMARC record found for the domain)

**## Production Guidance** -- How to write LinkedIn content. Cover:
- Hook first: the first line is everything. It must appear before the "see more" truncation (~210 characters for mobile). Do NOT start with "I" -- use a question, bold claim, counterintuitive statement, or data point.
- Native content only: LinkedIn algorithm penalizes posts with external links (~60% less reach per 2026 data). Content must stand alone without requiring a click. If a link is essential, place it in the first comment, not the post body. Per CONTEXT.md specific idea: explicitly address LinkedIn's preference for native content over links.
- Visual assets: document/carousel posts and image posts get higher engagement than text-only. If including images, use 1:1 or 4:5 ratio.
- Engagement design: end with a question or conversation prompt. Consider what the reader would comment. The algorithm rewards posts that generate meaningful replies.
- Format for scanning: use line breaks liberally. Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). Use whitespace as a design element.

**## Discipline Gates** -- 4 gates:

DISC-LI-01: Hook Quality -- Tier 1
- Checks: first line effectiveness and format
- Against: post content
- Criteria 1: Opening word (PASS: post does NOT start with "I" -- opens with question, data, claim, or scene-setting, WARN: post starts with "I" but follows immediately with a surprising or counterintuitive statement, FAIL: post starts with "I" followed by mundane context like "I was thinking" or "I recently")
- Criteria 2: Hook visibility (PASS: hook is complete within first 210 characters (visible before "see more" on mobile), WARN: hook starts strong but key payoff falls after the 210-char truncation point, FAIL: first 210 characters are setup/context with no hook)

DISC-LI-02: Native Content Format -- Tier 1
- Checks: absence of external links in post body
- Against: post content
- Criteria 1: Link placement (PASS: no external URLs in the main post body, WARN: one external link present in post body but post content stands alone without it, FAIL: external link is the primary purpose of the post -- link-centric content)
- Criteria 2: Content self-sufficiency (PASS: reader gets full value from the post text alone without clicking any link, WARN: post summarizes linked content but adds original perspective, FAIL: post is a teaser that requires clicking a link for the actual content)

DISC-LI-03: Engagement Path -- Tier 2
- Checks: conversation prompt and reply path design
- Against: post content
- Criteria 1: Conversation prompt (PASS: post ends with a specific question or clear invitation to respond, WARN: post has an implied question but no explicit ask, FAIL: post ends without any engagement prompt -- just a statement or link)
- Criteria 2: Reply path clarity (PASS: the question/prompt has a clear, easy-to-answer response path -- readers know what to type, WARN: prompt exists but is too broad ("What do you think?"), FAIL: N/A -- linked to conversation prompt)

DISC-LI-04: Character/Format Limits -- Tier 2
- Checks: post length and formatting for optimal engagement
- Against: LinkedIn platform limits and engagement data
- Criteria 1: Character count (PASS: post is 1,200-2,100 characters (optimal engagement range), WARN: post is 800-1,199 or 2,101-3,000 characters, FAIL: post exceeds 3,000 characters or is under 300 characters)
- Criteria 2: Readability formatting (PASS: line breaks between paragraphs, short paragraphs of 1-3 sentences, limited hashtags (3-5), WARN: some long paragraphs or excessive hashtags (6-10), FAIL: wall of text with no line breaks, or 10+ hashtags)

**## Base Gate Overrides:**
None -- all base gates keep default tiers.

- Hashtags: 3-5 maximum, placed at end of post
- Mentions: use sparingly, mention people who will engage
- Carousel documents: PDF format, 1:1 or 4:5 ratio slides

**## Production Guidance** -- How to write social content, with platform-specific subsections per CONTEXT.md specific idea:

### General Social Principles
- Native format preference: each platform rewards native content (text, image, video) over link posts
- Platform voice: adapt tone per platform while maintaining brand voice. X is concise and direct, Instagram is visual-first with descriptive captions, Facebook allows longer narrative
- One idea per post: social posts succeed by delivering one clear point, not summarizing multiple ideas

### X/Twitter Production Rules
- Maximum value in minimum words. Target under 200 characters for highest engagement even though limit is 280.
- No rhetorical questions per CONTEXT.md PLAY-06 -- X algorithm deprioritizes them. Use statements, data points, or direct challenges instead.
- Thread-first for longer content: if the idea needs more than 280 chars, write as a thread with each tweet standalone-valuable.

### Instagram Production Rules
- Visual-first: the image or carousel IS the content. Caption supports and extends the visual.
- Carousel ratio: 1:1 (feed-optimized) or 4:5 (maximum vertical space). 10 slides max.
- Caption structure: hook in first line (before "more" truncation), value in body, CTA and hashtags at end.

### Facebook Production Rules
- Text before media: Facebook shows text above images/videos. Lead with the hook.
- Longer narrative acceptable: 100-500 words perform well. First 100 words visible before truncation.
- Community engagement: ask questions, run polls, invite shares.

**## Discipline Gates** -- 4 gates with platform-aware criteria:

- Criteria 3: Facebook limit (PASS: under 1,500 characters with key message in first 100 words, WARN: 1,500-2,000 characters, FAIL: over 2,000 characters or key message buried after first 200 words. N/A if not Facebook.)

DISC-SOC-02: Native Format Preference -- Tier 2
- Checks: content formatted for native platform consumption
- Against: platform best practices

- Criteria 2: Media format (PASS: images/video in platform-preferred ratios (Instagram 1:1 or 4:5, Facebook 1.91:1 for link previews, X 16:9 or 1:1), WARN: media present but non-standard ratio, FAIL: N/A -- media is optional on some platforms. - Criteria 3: Facebook text-before-media (PASS: text appears above any image/video with hook in first sentence, WARN: text present but hook is weak or buried, FAIL: image/video posted with no or minimal text (under 20 characters). N/A if not Facebook.)
