**Frontmatter:**
```yaml
---
discipline: youtube
asset_types: [video-script, thumbnail-brief, description, community-post]
version: "1.0"
---
```

**Section 1 - Production Guidance:** Cover hook in first 5 seconds (PLAY-04 requirement), title+thumbnail click-fit (title promises, thumbnail amplifies visually), description SEO (target keyword in first 2 lines, timestamps, links), end-screen CTA placement, retention curve awareness (front-load value).

**Section 2 - Discipline Gates (6 gates):**

### DISC-YOUTUBE-01: Hook Strength -- Tier 1
Checks: First 5 seconds of video script contain a specific promise, question, or pattern interrupt.
- PASS: Script opens with a concrete hook (problem statement, bold claim, or curiosity gap) within the first 3 sentences
- WARN: Script has an opening but it is generic or brand-focused rather than audience-focused
- FAIL: Script opens with an introduction, greeting, or sponsor mention before the hook

### DISC-YOUTUBE-02: Title-Thumbnail Click-Fit -- Tier 1
Checks: Title and thumbnail brief work as a complementary pair (title promises, thumbnail visualizes).
- PASS: Title contains a specific promise/curiosity gap AND thumbnail brief describes a contrasting visual element that amplifies (not duplicates) the title
- WARN: Title and thumbnail overlap in message (both say the same thing)
- FAIL: Title is generic/clickbait with no matching thumbnail concept, OR thumbnail brief is missing

### DISC-YOUTUBE-03: Description SEO -- Tier 2
Checks: Description includes target keyword in first 2 lines, timestamps, and relevant links.
- PASS: Target keyword appears in first 150 characters, timestamps present for 10+ min videos, at least 2 links included
- WARN: Keyword present but not in first 150 chars, OR timestamps missing for long-form content
- FAIL: No target keyword in description, or description is under 100 characters

### DISC-YOUTUBE-04: Retention Structure -- Tier 2
Checks: Script front-loads value and avoids retention killers.
- PASS: Key value proposition delivered within first 30% of script, pattern interrupts every 2-3 minutes, no extended tangents
- WARN: Value delivered but after a long preamble (>20% of script)
- FAIL: Script buries the main point past the halfway mark or has no structural variety

### DISC-YOUTUBE-05: End-Screen CTA -- Tier 2
Checks: Script includes a clear end-screen call-to-action.
- PASS: Final section includes specific CTA (subscribe, watch next, link) with verbal and visual placement notes
- WARN: CTA exists but is generic ("like and subscribe")
- FAIL: No end-screen CTA in the script

### DISC-YOUTUBE-06: Thumbnail Contrast -- Tier 2
Checks: Thumbnail brief specifies high-contrast, low-clutter visual design.
- PASS: Thumbnail brief specifies max 3 elements, contrasting colors, readable text at mobile size, face/emotion if applicable
- WARN: Thumbnail brief exists but has more than 3 text elements or no contrast specification
- FAIL: No thumbnail brief, or brief describes a busy/cluttered design

**Section 3 - Base Gate Overrides:**
| Base Gate | Override | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| GATE-10 (Format Correctness) | Tier 1 | Video scripts have strict format requirements (timestamps, shot notes) that directly affect production |

**Section 4 - Format Rules:** Video script format (scene markers, B-roll notes, lower-third callouts), thumbnail brief format (3-element max, color spec, text overlay), description template (keyword line, timestamps, links, hashtags), community post format (poll/text/image spec).

**Section 5 - Examples:** One strong hook example, one weak hook example. One good title+thumbnail pair, one bad pair.

**Section 6 - Anti-Patterns:** Opening with "Hey guys", burying the hook after a sponsor read, thumbnail text duplicating the title verbatim, description with no keywords or links, scripts without retention markers.

**Section 7 - Metrics:** Views, watch time, average view duration, CTR (impressions to views), subscriber conversion, end-screen click rate.

Create playbooks/paid-ads.md (target 250-350 lines) with:

**Frontmatter:**
```yaml
---
discipline: paid-ads
asset_types: [search-ad, display-ad, social-ad, video-ad, landing-page-ad]
version: "1.0"
---
```

**Section 1 - Production Guidance:** Ad-to-landing-page message match (PLAY-08 requirement), creative variety across ad sets, audience-creative fit (match creative angle to audience segment), bid strategy awareness (budget/bid approach affects creative), platform-specific copy constraints (Google Ads character limits, Meta primary text, LinkedIn single image).

**Section 2 - Discipline Gates (5 gates):**

### DISC-PAID-ADS-01: Message Match -- Tier 1
Checks: Ad copy promise matches the landing page headline and above-the-fold content.
- PASS: Ad headline claim appears verbatim or semantically equivalent in the landing page H1, and the CTA matches the landing page primary action
- WARN: Ad and landing page share the same topic but the specific promise differs (e.g., ad says "50% off" but LP says "save big")
- FAIL: Ad copy makes a promise not present on the landing page, or landing page URL is missing from the brief

### DISC-PAID-ADS-02: Creative Variety -- Tier 1
Checks: Campaign includes multiple creative variations for testing per PLAY-08.
- PASS: At least 3 distinct ad variations (different hooks, visuals, or angles) defined in the brief
- WARN: 2 variations defined
- FAIL: Single creative with no variation plan

### DISC-PAID-ADS-03: Audience-Creative Fit -- Tier 2
Checks: Creative angle matches the target audience segment's pain points and language.
- PASS: Ad copy uses language from ICP.md customer language library, addresses a specific pain point or JTBD relevant to the audience segment
- WARN: Ad copy addresses the right topic but uses generic rather than ICP-specific language
- FAIL: Ad copy uses internal/product jargon or addresses pain points not in the ICP definition

### DISC-PAID-ADS-04: Platform Format Compliance -- Tier 2
Checks: Ad copy respects platform character limits and format rules.
- PASS: All headlines under platform limit (Google: 30 chars, Meta: 40 chars), descriptions under limit (Google: 90 chars, Meta: 125 chars primary text), image specs match platform requirements
- WARN: Copy is within 10% of limits but not optimized for truncation
- FAIL: Copy exceeds platform character limits or uses formats unsupported by the target platform

### DISC-PAID-ADS-05: Bid Strategy Alignment -- Tier 2
Checks: Creative approach aligns with the stated bid strategy and campaign objective.
- PASS: Awareness campaigns use broad/engagement-focused creative, conversion campaigns use direct-response creative with clear CTA, the brief explicitly states bid strategy
- WARN: Bid strategy stated but creative style does not clearly match the objective
- FAIL: No bid strategy stated in the brief, or creative contradicts the campaign objective (e.g., brand awareness copy with aggressive CTA)

**Section 3 - Base Gate Overrides:**
| Base Gate | Override | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| GATE-05 (Funnel Integrity) | Tier 1 | Paid traffic with broken funnels wastes ad spend directly |
| GATE-06 (UTM Hygiene) | Tier 1 | Paid channels require precise attribution to justify spend |

**Section 4 - Format Rules:** Google Search Ads (headline 1-3, description 1-2, char limits), Meta/Facebook Ads (primary text, headline, description, image specs), LinkedIn Ads (single image, carousel, text), display ad sizes (300x250, 728x90, 160x600), video ad specs (6s bumper, 15s, 30s).

**Section 5 - Examples:** One strong message-match pair (ad + landing page), one weak pair. One varied creative set, one monotone set.

**Section 6 - Anti-Patterns:** Sending all ad traffic to the homepage, single creative with no A/B variants, awareness creative with conversion bid strategy, ignoring platform character limits, using the same copy across all platforms.

**Section 7 - Metrics:** CPC, CTR, conversion rate, ROAS, CPA, quality score (Google), relevance score (Meta), impression share.
