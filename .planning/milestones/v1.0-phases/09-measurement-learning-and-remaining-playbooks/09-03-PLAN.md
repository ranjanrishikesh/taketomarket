Create playbooks/affiliate.md (target 250-350 lines) with:

**Frontmatter:**
```yaml
---
discipline: affiliate
asset_types: [affiliate-creative-kit, affiliate-landing-page, affiliate-email-swipe]
version: "1.0"
---
```

**Section 1 - Production Guidance:** Creative kit completeness (banners, email swipes, social copy, landing page copy), attribution/cookie logic awareness (cookie duration, first-touch vs last-touch), commission structure sanity (LTV/CAC math per PLAY-09), partner enablement (affiliates need enough context to sell without misrepresenting), compliance guardrails for affiliate claims.

**Section 2 - Discipline Gates (5 gates):**

### DISC-AFFILIATE-01: Creative Kit Completeness -- Tier 1
Checks: Creative kit includes all required asset types for affiliates per PLAY-09.
- PASS: Kit includes at least: 3 banner sizes, 2 email swipe templates, social copy snippets, landing page copy with tracking links
- WARN: Kit has 2 of 4 asset categories
- FAIL: Kit has fewer than 2 asset categories or no tracking links included

### DISC-AFFILIATE-02: Attribution Logic -- Tier 1
Checks: Cookie duration, attribution model, and tracking are specified per PLAY-09.
- PASS: Creative kit specifies cookie duration (e.g., 30-day, 90-day), attribution model (first-touch or last-touch), and all links use proper tracking parameters
- WARN: Tracking links present but cookie duration or attribution model not documented
- FAIL: No tracking infrastructure specified, or creative uses non-trackable links

### DISC-AFFILIATE-03: Commission Sanity -- Tier 2
Checks: Commission structure passes LTV/CAC math per PLAY-09.
- PASS: Commission percentage documented, LTV/CAC ratio calculated and shows positive unit economics (commission < customer LTV margin), payout terms stated
- WARN: Commission stated but LTV/CAC math not shown
- FAIL: No commission structure documented, or commission exceeds estimated customer LTV margin

### DISC-AFFILIATE-04: Claim Accuracy for Partners -- Tier 1
Checks: Affiliate copy does not make claims beyond what is approved in BRAND.md proof points.
- PASS: All claims in affiliate creative map to approved proof points, no income/results guarantees
- WARN: Claims are technically accurate but use amplified language not in BRAND.md
- FAIL: Creative includes unapproved claims, income guarantees, or unsubstantiated statistics

### DISC-AFFILIATE-05: Partner Enablement -- Tier 2
Checks: Affiliates have enough context to sell accurately without access to internal docs.
- PASS: Kit includes product summary, target audience description, key differentiators, approved messaging, and FAQ section
- WARN: Kit has product summary and messaging but missing FAQ or differentiators
- FAIL: Kit provides creative assets only with no product context or messaging guidance

**Section 3 - Base Gate Overrides:**
| Base Gate | Override | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| GATE-02 (Claim Accuracy) | Tier 1 | Affiliate claims are amplified by third parties; inaccurate claims create legal/brand risk |
| GATE-07 (Compliance) | Tier 1 | FTC requires affiliate disclosure; compliance is non-negotiable |

**Section 4 - Format Rules:** Banner specs (standard IAB sizes), email swipe format (subject lines, preview text, body with merge tags), social copy format (platform-specific lengths), landing page copy structure.

**Section 5 - Examples:** One complete creative kit (abbreviated), one incomplete kit.

**Section 6 - Anti-Patterns:** Providing creative without product context, income/results guarantees, ignoring FTC disclosure requirements, single banner size, non-trackable links.

**Section 7 - Metrics:** Affiliate-driven revenue, conversion rate by affiliate, EPC (earnings per click), active affiliate rate, creative usage rate, refund/chargeback rate.

**A. Create playbooks/pr-media.md (target 250-350 lines):**

**Frontmatter:**
```yaml
---
discipline: pr-media
asset_types: [press-release, media-pitch, media-kit, byline-article]
version: "1.0"
---
```

**Section 1 - Production Guidance:** Media list structure (per PLAY-10), pitch angle-per-journalist customization, embargo management, earned media measurement approach, press release structure (inverted pyramid, quotes, boilerplate), byline article positioning.

**Section 2 - Discipline Gates (5 gates):**

### DISC-PR-MEDIA-01: Media List Structure -- Tier 1
Checks: Media outreach includes a structured journalist/outlet list with angles per PLAY-10.
- PASS: Media list includes at least 10 contacts with outlet name, journalist name, beat, and a specific pitch angle per journalist
- WARN: Media list has contacts but uses the same generic pitch for all
- FAIL: No media list, or list has fewer than 5 contacts, or no pitch angles defined

### DISC-PR-MEDIA-02: Pitch Angle Specificity -- Tier 1
Checks: Each pitch is customized to the journalist's beat and recent coverage per PLAY-10.
- PASS: Pitch references the journalist's recent work or beat, connects the story to their audience's interests
- WARN: Pitch is topically relevant to the beat but does not reference specific recent coverage
- FAIL: Generic pitch with no journalist-specific customization

### DISC-PR-MEDIA-03: Embargo Management -- Tier 2
Checks: Embargo terms are documented and communicated if applicable per PLAY-10.
- PASS: Embargo date/time clearly stated in pitch, NDA or embargo agreement terms defined, break protocol documented
- WARN: Embargo mentioned but terms are vague (no specific date/time)
- FAIL: Embargoed information sent without embargo terms, or conflicting embargo dates across pitches

### DISC-PR-MEDIA-04: Press Release Structure -- Tier 2
Checks: Press release follows inverted pyramid with required elements.
- PASS: Includes headline, dateline, lead paragraph (who/what/when/where/why), supporting details, executive quote, boilerplate, media contact info
- WARN: Missing one element (e.g., boilerplate or media contact)
- FAIL: Missing headline, lead paragraph, or more than 2 required elements

### DISC-PR-MEDIA-05: Earned Media Measurement -- Tier 2
Checks: Coverage tracking and measurement plan exists per PLAY-10.
- PASS: Measurement plan specifies tracking method (media monitoring tool or manual), target outlets, share-of-voice baseline, and success criteria
- WARN: Success criteria defined but tracking method not specified
- FAIL: No measurement plan for earned media outcomes

**Section 3 - Base Gate Overrides:**
| Base Gate | Override | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| GATE-02 (Claim Accuracy) | Tier 1 | Press claims are amplified by journalists; inaccuracies become public record |

**Sections 4-7:** Format Rules (press release format, pitch email format, media kit contents, byline structure), Examples (strong pitch vs weak pitch), Anti-Patterns (spray-and-pray pitching, self-congratulatory press releases, ignoring journalist beat), Metrics (media mentions, share of voice, backlinks from coverage, referral traffic, tier 1/2/3 coverage ratio).

**B. Create playbooks/events.md (target 250-350 lines):**

**Frontmatter:**
```yaml
---
discipline: events
asset_types: [event-landing-page, webinar-script, sponsorship-brief, post-event-recap]
version: "1.0"
---
```

**Section 1 - Production Guidance:** Pre/during/post campaign phases (PLAY-11 requirement), webinar funnel structure (registration page -> reminder sequence -> live event -> recording follow-up), community building at events, sponsorship ROI calculation, content repurposing from event recordings.

**Section 2 - Discipline Gates (5 gates):**

### DISC-EVENTS-01: Campaign Phase Coverage -- Tier 1
Checks: Event campaign covers all 3 phases (pre, during, post) per PLAY-11.
- PASS: Brief defines distinct content/actions for pre-event (promotion, registration), during-event (engagement, live content), and post-event (follow-up, recording, recap)
- WARN: Two of three phases covered
- FAIL: Only one phase covered (typically just the event itself with no pre/post plan)

### DISC-EVENTS-02: Webinar Funnel Integrity -- Tier 1
Checks: Webinar has a complete registration-to-follow-up funnel per PLAY-11.
- PASS: Funnel includes registration page, confirmation email, reminder sequence (24h and 1h before), live event script, recording delivery, and post-event CTA
- WARN: Has registration and event but missing reminder sequence or post-event follow-up
- FAIL: No registration funnel defined, or event with no follow-up plan

### DISC-EVENTS-03: Sponsorship ROI -- Tier 2
Checks: Sponsorship brief includes ROI projection and success criteria per PLAY-11.
- PASS: Brief includes sponsorship cost, expected reach/leads, cost-per-lead projection, brand visibility metrics, and success/failure thresholds
- WARN: Cost and expected reach stated but no per-lead math
- FAIL: Sponsorship brief with no ROI projection or success criteria

### DISC-EVENTS-04: Community Building -- Tier 2
Checks: Event plan includes community engagement beyond the event itself per PLAY-11.
- PASS: Plan includes at least 2 community actions (e.g., Slack/Discord invite, LinkedIn group, follow-up discussion, user group formation)
- WARN: Single community touchpoint defined
- FAIL: No community building element in the event plan

### DISC-EVENTS-05: Content Repurposing Plan -- Tier 2
Checks: Event recording/materials have a repurposing plan for other channels.
- PASS: Plan specifies at least 3 derivative assets from event content (e.g., blog recap, social clips, email highlight, podcast episode)
- WARN: 1-2 derivative assets planned
- FAIL: No repurposing plan for event content

**Section 3 - Base Gate Overrides:**
| Base Gate | Override | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| GATE-05 (Funnel Integrity) | Tier 1 | Event funnels (registration -> attendance -> follow-up) are critical to ROI |

**Sections 4-7:** Format Rules (event landing page structure, webinar script format, sponsorship brief template, post-event recap format), Examples (complete 3-phase plan vs single-phase plan), Anti-Patterns (events with no follow-up, sponsorships with no ROI tracking, webinars without registration funnels), Metrics (registrations, attendance rate, engagement during event, post-event conversions, content repurposing output, sponsorship ROI).
